# Geecom Learning — 90-Day Organic Content Strategy

## Overview

This strategy covers the full organic channel stack for Geecom Learning: LinkedIn, email newsletter, and blog/SEO. The goal is not to be everywhere at once. It is to be consistently useful to the right audience — HR managers, operations directors, and people leaders in BC and across Canada who own the training problem without an internal L&D team.

The four outcomes this strategy serves:

1. Book discovery calls (pipeline)
2. Build brand awareness (reach)
3. Grow an email list (owned audience)
4. Position as a thought leader in workplace learning (trust)

These are not competing goals. A well-run organic programme achieves all four simultaneously, because the same content that educates a reader also builds trust, and trust is what converts a reader into a call booking.

---

## Brand Voice Principles

Every piece of content — LinkedIn post, email, blog — should sound like the same person wrote it.

- **Narrative-led:** Start with an observation, a story, or a moment before making the point.
- **Warm but evidence-backed:** You care about the reader's situation. You also know what works.
- **Plain language:** No jargon. No acronyms without explanation. No industry-insider language that loses a busy ops director at sentence two.
- **Employer-facing POV:** You are writing for the person who owns the training problem, not the learner.
- **Short punchy closing lines:** Earn the reader's attention, then land.
- **No emojis. No AI-template openers.** ("In today's fast-paced world..." is not how we open anything.)

## Channel 1: LinkedIn

### Why LinkedIn First

Geecom's buyers are on LinkedIn. HR directors, L&D managers, operations leaders, and CEOs of growing SMBs in BC and across Canada use LinkedIn professionally. It is where they build trust in vendors before they ever visit a website. Organic LinkedIn content compounds — a post from month one still shows up in search for months after.

### Posting Cadence

**Target: 3 posts per week**

- Monday: Insight or observation post (thought leadership)
- Wednesday: Story or case-based post (social proof, relatable problem)
- Friday: Practical tip or question post (engagement driver)

Start here. Once traction is established (connections 500+, regular comments), consider adding a fourth post mid-quarter.

### Content Pillars

Four pillars keep the feed coherent without being repetitive.

**Pillar 1 — The Problem (30% of posts)**
Posts that name the training frustrations Geecom's buyers live with every day. Freelancer chaos. Compliance anxiety. Onboarding that takes four months. These posts generate the most shares and comments from readers who recognise themselves.

**Pillar 2 — The Evidence (25% of posts)**
Data-backed posts about the real cost of bad training, the ROI of getting it right, and what research says about how people actually learn. These establish credibility and give readers something to think about.

**Pillar 3 — The Approach (25% of posts)**
Posts that show how Geecom thinks about the craft of learning design. Microlearning vs. long-form. Why compliance training fails. What makes onboarding videos actually get watched. This is thought leadership — not "here is our process," but "here is a better way to think about this."

**Pillar 4 — The Invitation (20% of posts)**
Softer posts that open a conversation. A question to the audience. A behind-the-scenes observation. These generate replies and start direct conversations.

### Engagement Rules

- Respond to every comment within 24 hours. Early comments boost distribution.
- Comment on 5–10 posts per week from peers, clients, and potential buyers. Genuine observations, not "great post."
- Connect with every person who engages with your content. Send a short, non-pitchy message.
- Do not auto-post. Write each post freshly, or at minimum read it aloud before scheduling.

### LinkedIn Post Format Rules

- **Hook:** First line must stand alone and create a reason to click "see more." Under 110 characters.
- **Body:** 800–1,000 characters total. One idea per post. No walls of text — use line breaks liberally.
- **Close:** One CTA question that invites a comment.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5 at the end. Use a mix of high-volume (#WorkplaceTraining, #HR) and niche (#eLearning, #ComplianceTraining, #L&D).
- **No links in the post itself.** LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links. Put the link in the first comment if you need it.

## Channel 2: Email Newsletter

### Why Build a List

A LinkedIn following is rented. An email list is owned. If LinkedIn changes its algorithm, your reach can drop overnight. An email subscriber has raised their hand and asked to hear from you — that is a fundamentally warmer audience.

### Opt-In Hook

The list should offer something worth subscribing for. Two approaches:

**Option A — The Resource:** A one-page PDF guide ("The 5 Questions to Ask Before Commissioning Any Training Content") offered on the website and LinkedIn.

**Option B — The Newsletter Promise:** A clear, specific promise: "One short email, once a month. Practical L&D insight for the people who own the training problem without a full team."

Start with Option B. It requires no additional asset and can be added as a signup block to the footer of every page.

### Send Cadence

**Monthly.** One well-crafted email per month beats four rushed ones. Readers learn to expect it. The goal is not frequency — it is reliability.

### Content Structure (Each Issue)

Each issue follows this structure:

1. **Opening line:** One observation or story from the month. 2–3 sentences.
2. **Main piece:** One useful idea in full. 300–400 words. Not a summary of a blog post — the actual substance.
3. **One link:** A single relevant resource (a new blog post, a useful external article, a case study). Track clicks, not opens.
4. **Closing CTA:** One clear action. Usually "book a call" or "reply to this email."

### Subject Line Approach

- Keep subject lines under 50 characters
- Avoid spam triggers: "free," "guaranteed," "click here," "limited time"
- The best subject lines are either specific ("Why compliance training fails at 92% completion") or intriguing ("The 35-second problem")
- A/B test when list is over 500 subscribers

## Channel 3: Blog / SEO

### The Opportunity

Geecom's buyers search Google when they have a training problem. "How much does eLearning development cost." "Should I outsource L&D." "Psychological health and safety in the workplace." These are not high-competition queries — they are mid-tail searches with commercial intent, and a well-written, long-form article can rank for them with minimal link-building.

### Target Keyword Clusters

Organised by service and buyer intent:

**Cluster 1 — eLearning Development**
- how much does elearning development cost
- elearning development for small business
- custom elearning vs off-the-shelf
- elearning development timeline

**Cluster 2 — Compliance Training**
- compliance training for employees
- workplace compliance training Canada
- how to make compliance training not boring
- WHMIS / OSHA / harassment training requirements

**Cluster 3 — Onboarding**
- employee onboarding best practices
- onboarding video production
- how long should onboarding take
- cost of poor onboarding

**Cluster 4 — Psychological Health & Safety**
- psychological health and safety in the workplace
- mental health at work employer obligations
- how to create a psychologically safe workplace
- psychological safety and productivity

**Cluster 5 — L&D Strategy**
- should you outsource learning and development
- in-house vs outsourced L&D
- l&d strategy for small business
- how to build a training programme

**Cluster 6 — Microlearning**
- what is microlearning
- microlearning examples for business
- microlearning vs elearning

### Publication Cadence

**One well-researched article per month.** Quality over volume. A 1,500-word article that genuinely answers a question will outperform five thin 400-word pieces.

### On-Page SEO Basics for Each Article

- H1 includes the target keyword exactly
- Meta description under 160 characters
- At least 3 internal links per article
- At least 2 external links to authoritative sources
- Article CTA links to contact.html
- Structured data (Article schema) in the head
