# Geecom Learning — Cold Email Sequence
## Compliance Training Outreach | North America SMBs

**How to use this sequence:**
- Send from a subdomain (e.g., outreach@mail.geecomlearning.com — never your main domain until warmed)
- Personalise the [BRACKET] fields for every contact before sending
- Space emails as indicated — do not compress the timeline
- Verify every email address through NeverBounce or ZeroBounce before sending
- Stop all follow-ups the moment someone replies or books a call

---

## Email 1 — Day 1 (The Opener)
**Purpose:** Relevance check. Get a yes/no, not a sale.

**Subject options (A/B test these):**
- `compliance training — [Company]`
- `[Company]'s [HIPAA / OSHA / FINRA] training`
- `quick question for [First Name]`

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Noticed [Company] has been growing quickly — [personalised trigger: e.g., "you opened a new location in [City]" / "you're hiring [role] right now" / "you recently brought on [X] new practices"].

We make short compliance and onboarding training videos for [industry] teams — the kind that actually get watched, and that you can track and certify.

Most of our clients were relying on PDFs or in-person sessions before working with us. The switch usually takes 4–6 weeks and covers [HIPAA + OSHA / FINRA / OSHA 10] across your whole team.

Is this on your radar, or not a priority right now?

[Your name]
Geecom Learning | geecomlearning.com

---

**Notes:**
- Under 100 words — do not add more
- Do NOT include links, attachments, or a calendar link in email 1
- The last line is a yes/no question — make it easy to say "not now"
- Personalise the trigger to the specific signal you found (hiring post, acquisition, expansion, etc.)

---

## Email 2 — Day 4 (The Follow-Up)
**Purpose:** Stay visible. Don't oversell.

**Subject:** `Re: compliance training — [Company]`
(Reply to the same thread — keeps context visible)

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried.

We typically work with [healthcare groups / financial advisory firms / construction companies] in the 50–500 employee range — teams that have a real compliance obligation but no internal L&D team to manage it.

If the timing is off, no problem at all. Happy to check back in a few months.

[Your name]

---

**Notes:**
- This is a one-paragraph nudge, not a resell
- If they say "not now," ask "when would be a better time?" and set a calendar reminder
- Do not add new information — this email is purely a soft ping

---

## Email 3 — Day 10 (The Value Add)
**Purpose:** Offer something without asking for anything.

**Subject:** `one thing that helps with [HIPAA / OSHA / FINRA] training at scale`

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Whether we end up working together or not, thought this might be useful:

The most common compliance training failure we see in [industry] is completion rates — not the content itself. Staff watch once, the record gets lost, and when a regulator asks for documentation, nobody can produce it.

We build every module with a completion certificate and audit trail built in — so if you're ever inspected, you can pull the record for any employee in under a minute.

If you'd like to see a sample from a [healthcare / financial / construction] client, I'm happy to share one. No pitch attached.

[Your name]
Geecom Learning | geecomlearning.com

---

**Notes:**
- This email gives before it asks — the "sample" offer is low friction
- Personalise the compliance failure to their industry (HIPAA audit, OSHA inspection, FINRA review)
- If they respond to this email asking to see a sample, send them your portfolio at geecomlearning.com — and book a call

---

## Email 4 — Day 18 (The Graceful Exit)
**Purpose:** Close the loop with dignity. Leave the door open.

**Subject:** `closing the loop`

---

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I've sent a few notes your way — I'll stop following up now so I'm not crowding your inbox.

If [Company]'s compliance training situation changes — a new hire surge, an upcoming audit, a new location opening — feel free to reach out. We're usually able to turn a full programme around in 4–6 weeks.

You can find us at geecomlearning.com or email hello@geecomlearning.com any time.

Wishing you a good [quarter / year],

[Your name]
Geecom Learning

---

**Notes:**
- Never send more than 4 touches to a cold prospect
- This email has a surprisingly high reply rate — people respond to being treated respectfully
- The "4-6 week" timeline plants a seed for future urgency (an audit deadline, a new hire event)
- After this, move the contact to a nurture list (monthly newsletter or LinkedIn connection)

## Deliverability Checklist (Do Before Sending Any Email)

- [ ] Set up SPF, DKIM, DMARC on outreach subdomain
- [ ] Warm up sending domain for 3–4 weeks (10–20 emails/day ramping to 50)
- [ ] Verify every email address through NeverBounce or ZeroBounce
- [ ] Include your physical mailing address in every email footer (CASL / CAN-SPAM)
- [ ] Include a one-click unsubscribe or "reply STOP" instruction
- [ ] Never send more than 50 cold emails per day per inbox
- [ ] No links in Email 1 — wait until Email 3
- [ ] No attachments in any cold email
- [ ] Avoid words: guaranteed, free, click here, limited time, act now, special offer
