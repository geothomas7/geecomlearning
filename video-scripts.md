# Geecom Learning — TikTok / Reels Video Scripts
## 3 Production-Ready Scene Briefs

**Usage notes:**
- Each script is 4 scenes, 6–8 seconds per scene, 24–30 seconds total
- Format: visual prompt / text overlay / voiceover line / duration
- Hand to a video editor or drop into an AI video tool (Runway, Pika, HeyGen, etc.)
- No music specified — choose something calm, not upbeat, that matches the thoughtful tone
- On-screen speaker or narrator should feel genuine, not polished-presenter
- Captions should be on by default (most views are silent)

## Script 1 — Psychological Health in the Workplace

**Title:** "Your safety plan has a blind spot"
**Target platform:** LinkedIn, Instagram Reels, TikTok
**Total duration:** 26 seconds

---

### Scene 1 — 6 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Wide shot of a busy open-plan office. People at their desks, a few walking by. Looks ordinary. Calm. Slightly sterile.

**Text overlay:**
"Most workplaces have a physical safety plan."

**Voiceover:**
"Most workplaces have a physical safety plan."

---

### Scene 2 — 6 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Close shot of a hand-written sign: "Report all hazards." Cut to: a hard hat on a hook. Cut to: a fire extinguisher on a wall. Quick edits.

**Text overlay:**
"Slip hazard. Fire exit. First aid kit."

**Voiceover:**
"Slip hazard. Fire exit. First aid kit. All covered."

---

### Scene 3 — 7 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Cut to: a single person sitting at a desk alone, late in the day. Office is emptying. They're staring at a screen. Tired. The image is a contrast to the earlier busy scene.

**Text overlay:**
"But what about the hazards you can't see?"

**Voiceover:**
"But what about the hazards you can't see? Burnout. Harassment. The fear of speaking up."

---

### Scene 4 — 7 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Cut to: a manager and employee having what looks like a genuine, open conversation — not a formal meeting, more like a hallway check-in. Both relaxed. Close on their expressions.

**Text overlay:**
"Psychological health and safety is your legal and moral responsibility."

**Voiceover:**
"Psychological health and safety is not a nice-to-have. It is a legal obligation. And it starts with training your leaders."

**End card:**
"Geecom Learning | geecomlearning.com | Build training that actually works."

## Script 2 — The Real Cost of Poor Onboarding

**Title:** "What bad onboarding actually costs"
**Target platform:** LinkedIn, Instagram Reels, TikTok
**Total duration:** 28 seconds

---

### Scene 1 — 6 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
A new employee in a chair across from an HR desk. Paperwork on the table. Looking slightly overwhelmed. First day energy — slightly anxious.

**Text overlay:**
"Day 1. Your new hire is full of goodwill."

**Voiceover:**
"Day one. Your new hire is full of goodwill."

---

### Scene 2 — 7 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
A series of quick cuts: the same person looking confused at a screen, asking a colleague a question and getting a vague shrug, looking at a pile of printed manuals nobody has touched.

**Text overlay:**
"Day 30. They're figuring it out alone."

**Voiceover:**
"But by day thirty, they're figuring it out alone. No structure. No role-specific context. Just: 'ask anyone if you need something.'"

---

### Scene 3 — 8 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Cut to: a calendar. Months 1, 2, 3 pass quickly. Month 6: a resignation letter sitting on a desk. A manager looking deflated. A "Now Hiring" sign appearing.

**Text overlay:**
"Replacing that person costs 50% of their annual salary."

**Voiceover:**
"And then they leave. And replacing that person costs about fifty percent of their annual salary. Recruiting, lost productivity, starting over."

---

### Scene 4 — 7 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Cut to: a short, clear onboarding video playing on a laptop. Someone watching it and nodding. A manager walking over, checking in. Looks structured, calm, intentional.

**Text overlay:**
"Structured onboarding improves 3-year retention by 82%."

**Voiceover:**
"Structured onboarding improves three-year retention by eighty-two percent. That is not a soft benefit. That is a financial return."

**End card:**
"Geecom Learning | geecomlearning.com | Build training that actually works."

## Script 3 — What Microlearning Actually Is

**Title:** "Microlearning — the short answer"
**Target platform:** LinkedIn, Instagram Reels, TikTok
**Total duration:** 27 seconds

---

### Scene 1 — 6 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Close shot of someone reluctantly clicking through a long, text-heavy eLearning slide. Jaw slightly dropped. Scroll bar at the top showing they're only 10% through.

**Text overlay:**
"You've sat through this training. You forgot it by Friday."

**Voiceover:**
"You've sat through this training. You forgot it by Friday."

---

### Scene 2 — 7 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
A simple graphic: a brain with a timer. Content going in at once, most of it visually leaking out. The text appears: "One long session = low retention."

**Text overlay:**
"The brain doesn't store information delivered all at once."

**Voiceover:**
"It's not your team's fault. The brain doesn't retain information delivered all at once. It retains information delivered over time."

---

### Scene 3 — 7 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
A sequence of quick clips: a 4-minute module on a phone, Monday morning. Same person, 4 minutes, Wednesday. Same person again, the following week. Small doses, spaced out.

**Text overlay:**
"4 minutes. Three times. Spaced out. That's microlearning."

**Voiceover:**
"Microlearning delivers one thing at a time, in small doses, spaced across days or weeks. That is how retention actually works."

---

### Scene 4 — 7 seconds

**Visual prompt:**
Cut to: a confident employee handling a real work situation — a customer question, a safety check, a compliance procedure. Handling it without hesitation.

**Text overlay:**
"What they learned — they kept."

**Voiceover:**
"When the moment comes to use what they learned — they actually have it. That is the difference microlearning makes."

**End card:**
"Geecom Learning | geecomlearning.com | Build training that actually works."
