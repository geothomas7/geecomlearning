Specialist Trainer Sourcing

We brief, source, vet, and place specialist trainers for Canadian corporates — statisticians, regulatory experts, and niche operational instructors — for one-off workshops and short engagements.

When the training you need is a person, not a course

Sometimes the training you need is not a module we can build. It is a person — a working statistician, a former regulator, a seasoned compliance specialist — in a room with your team for a day or two. We find that person, vet them, and handle the contracting.

Hunting for a trainer with the right credentials takes time most Human Resources (HR) and operations teams do not have. The candidates worth hiring are usually not on a marketplace. They sit inside government statistical services, regulatory bodies, university executive education arms, and private expert networks. Each source has its own contact pathway, its own quoting logic, and its own availability calendar.

You give us a brief. We come back with a shortlist of vetted candidates and a placement plan. If we cannot find a trainer who genuinely fits the brief, we say so and there is no fee.

Best for

  • One-off workshops on specialist topics
  • Short engagements of one to five days
  • Topics where credentials and pedigree matter
  • Corporate teams without an internal Learning & Development function
  • Briefs you have already tried to fill yourself
How it works

From brief to a trainer in the room

01

Brief

We sit down with you for thirty to forty-five minutes. Audience, topic, format, dates, budget envelope, and what a good trainer looks like for this specific session. No template form to fill in beforehand.

02

Source

We identify candidates from the right channels — institutional training services, university executive education, expert networks, and our own contacts in the Canadian Learning & Development community. We do not rely on a single roster.

03

Vet

We verify credentials, check recent work, confirm relevant corporate teaching experience, and have a working call with each shortlisted trainer. You meet the final two or three, not the long list.

04

Place

We handle the contract and the logistics — scheduling, materials, pre-session calls with you, invoicing, and a debrief afterwards. The trainer arrives prepared. You stay focused on the rest of your job.

What we source for

Topic categories

Examples of the briefs we take on. The list is illustrative, not exhaustive — if your topic is not here, ask.

Statistics, data literacy, and analytics

Trainers from federal statistics agencies, university statistics faculties, and applied research institutes. Sessions can range from foundational data literacy for non-analysts to advanced workshops on statistical inference, survey methodology, or predictive analytics.

Regulatory and compliance specialists

Former regulators and sector-specific compliance practitioners. Useful when you need someone who has worked inside the regulator that governs your industry, not just someone who has read the rulebook.

Sector and operational training

Technical instructors with worksite or workforce experience in your industry — manufacturing, energy, transportation, healthcare operations, public sector. Subject expertise paired with real corporate teaching credibility.

Leadership and people management

Vetted facilitators with a track record of delivering corporate workshops on management fundamentals, performance conversations, change leadership, and team effectiveness. We screen for delivery quality, not just credentials on paper.

Technical upskilling and emerging topics

Cybersecurity awareness, applied artificial intelligence (AI) literacy, accessibility, environmental, social and governance (ESG) reporting, privacy and data protection. Specialists who actively work in the field, not generalist trainers reading from a deck.

Custom briefs

Niche topics where you need someone we have to actively go and find — a former chief of a specific regulatory programme, a recognised academic in a narrow research area, or an operator from a specific institution. If they exist and they take corporate work, we will find them.

Where this service fits — and where it does not

This service is built specifically for corporate, workforce, and worksite training. It is not a general “find me an instructor” service.

We work with employers — companies and public sector organisations — sourcing trainers to deliver workplace learning to their staff. The brief is always tied to a workplace skill, a regulatory requirement, or a strategic capability the organisation needs to build.

If your need sits outside that scope, we will say so plainly at the briefing call and, where we can, point you to a more appropriate channel.

Not a fit for

  • Personal training, fitness, or wellness coaching for individuals
  • Sourcing instructors for university degree programmes or accredited certifications
  • Long-term staffing or permanent hiring of an in-house trainer
  • Self-serve marketplace browsing — we do not publish a public roster
  • Train-the-trainer programmes for your existing internal trainers
How fees work

Quoted per engagement, contingent on placement

Sourcing is quoted per engagement. The two common arrangements are a placement fee charged as a percentage of the trainer’s contracted fee, or a flat sourcing fee for harder-to-find specialists. Trainer fees themselves are set by the trainer or their institution — we do not mark them up silently. Our sourcing fee is explained in writing before any work starts and is contingent on a successful placement: if we cannot find a trainer who fits, you do not pay.

Common questions

Trainer sourcing FAQs

How is this different from a marketplace like Catalant or Upwork?

We are not a self-serve platform. You give us a brief and we go and find the right specialist for you, vet them, and place them. There is no public roster to search and no profiles to scroll. Each engagement is bespoke, and the sourcing channels we use — government statistical services, regulatory bodies, university executive education arms, expert networks — are not searchable in one place anyway.

Who pays the trainer — Geecom or us?

Either model works. Most clients prefer that we coordinate the contract and the invoice. We pay the trainer and bill you a single invoice with our sourcing fee shown transparently. For some engagements you can contract the trainer directly and we invoice only our placement fee. We tell you which arrangement makes more sense at the briefing call.

How long does sourcing take?

Common topics — data literacy, regulatory compliance, leadership facilitation — can usually be matched within one to two weeks from brief to confirmed shortlist. Niche briefs, such as a former chief of a specific regulatory programme or a recognised academic in a narrow research area, can take three to six weeks depending on availability. We give you a realistic timeline at the briefing stage.

What happens if you cannot find a suitable trainer?

You pay nothing. Our sourcing fee is contingent on a successful placement. If we cannot find a trainer who genuinely fits the brief, we tell you honestly and there is no charge. We would rather decline a brief than place the wrong specialist — a poor session damages your credibility with the audience and ours with you.

Have a brief in mind?

Send us the audience, the topic, the dates, and what a good trainer looks like. We will tell you within two business days whether it is a brief we can source — and how long it would take.
