**The problem we solve**

**H2: When the training you need is a person, not a course**

Sometimes what your team needs isn't a module we can build. It's a qualified instructor with real subject matter depth, someone who has done the work, not just studied it, standing in front of your people for a day.

Finding that person takes time. The trainers worth hiring aren't listed on a freelancer marketplace. They work inside regulatory bodies, research institutions, professional associations, and specialist consultancies. Each one has a different way of being reached, a different way of quoting, and a different lead time.

You give us a brief. We come back with a shortlist of vetted candidates and a placement plan. If we can't find someone who fits, there's no fee.

**Best for (sidebar):**
- One-off workshops on specialist topics
- Short engagements, one to five days
- Topics where the trainer's qualifications and practical experience matter
- Teams without an internal L&D function
- Briefs you've already tried to fill yourself

**How it works**

**H2: From brief to trainer in the room**

**01 Brief** — We spend thirty to forty-five minutes understanding what you need. Audience, topic, format, dates, budget, and what "qualified" looks like for this particular session.

**02 Source** — We reach out through the right channels for the brief. That might be a professional association, an institutional training office, a specialist consultancy, or someone in our own network. We don't rely on a single roster.

**03 Vet** — We verify qualifications, check recent delivery experience, and have a working call with each shortlisted candidate. You meet the final two or three, not the long list.

**04 Place** — We handle the contract, the scheduling, pre-session coordination, and invoicing. The trainer arrives prepared. You stay focused on everything else.

Examples of the briefs we take on. If your topic isn't listed, ask.

**FAQ**

**How is this different from a marketplace like Catalant or Upwork?**
We're not a self-serve platform. You give us a brief and we go find the right person, vet them, and place them. There's no roster to browse. The sourcing channels we use aren't searchable in one place, which is the whole reason this service exists.

**Who pays the trainer?**
Either arrangement works. Most clients prefer that we coordinate the contract and send a single invoice with our sourcing fee shown separately. For some engagements you can contract the trainer directly and we invoice only the placement fee. We'll recommend whichever makes more sense at the briefing call.

**How long does sourcing take?**
Common topics can usually be matched within one to two weeks. Niche briefs where we need a very specific profile can take three to six weeks. We'll give you a realistic timeline at the briefing stage.

**What happens if you can't find a suitable trainer?**
You pay nothing. If we can't find someone who fits the brief, we say so and there's no charge. We'd rather turn down a brief than place the wrong person.
