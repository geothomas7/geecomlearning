How we work

A clear path from idea to launch

No surprises, no scope creep. Every project follows the same four-step process — so you always know what's happening and what's coming next.

1

Discover

We start by understanding your audience, goals, and constraints — before we touch a single slide or script. This is where we ask the questions that prevent rework later: Who are the learners? What do they need to do differently? What already exists? What's the deadline and budget?

2

Design

With the discovery complete, we map out the curriculum, assessments, and delivery format. You'll review and approve a storyboard or outline before we build anything — so there are no surprises in the final product. Format, tone, and branding are all locked in here.

3

Build

Development happens in structured review rounds — typically two. You'll see work-in-progress at each checkpoint and provide feedback before we move forward. This keeps the project on track and ensures your input shapes the outcome, not just the final review.

4

Launch & Improve

We package and deliver the final content — SCORM/xAPI files, video exports, LMS upload support — whatever your deployment needs. Post-launch, we can help you monitor completion data and iterate on content based on real learner feedback.

Common questions

Things people usually ask before starting

How long does a typical project take?

A standard eLearning module (30–45 min) takes 6–10 weeks from kickoff to delivery. Onboarding videos are typically faster — 3–5 weeks. Timeline depends on content complexity and how quickly feedback comes in.

Do I need to provide all the content?

No — we can work from raw source material (policies, existing slides, SME interviews) and structure it for learning. The more you can share upfront, the smoother the Discover phase goes.

What if we need changes after launch?

We offer ongoing support for updates and revisions. Many clients keep us on retainer to refresh content as policies or products change.

Can you work with our existing LMS?

Yes. We package content to SCORM 1.2, SCORM 2004, or xAPI — whichever your LMS requires. We test before delivery so you're not troubleshooting on upload day.
